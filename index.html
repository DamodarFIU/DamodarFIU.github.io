Florida International University (FIU), Miami, FL

I am a Ph.D. student in Economics at Florida International University. My research spans macroeconomics and development economics. In macroeconomics, I study how global shocks — including commodity price fluctuations, demand shocks, and uncertainty — transmit to domestic inflation across heterogeneous economies, using Bayesian Structural VAR models. In development economics, I examine how overseas remittances shape food security and household welfare among smallholder households in Nepal, using shift-share instrumental variable strategies and panel data methods.

Working Papers

Global Shocks and Domestic Inflation: Evidence from 45 Economies
Damodar Gautam
Working Paper, 2025
This paper examines how global economic shocks — commodity price shocks, global demand shocks, and uncertainty shocks — transmit to domestic inflation across 45 heterogeneous economies. Using a Bayesian Structural VAR (B-SVAR) framework augmented with novel global uncertainty indices, I document significant heterogeneity in transmission driven by trade openness, financial development, central bank independence, exchange rate regimes, and commodity exposure.

Overseas Remittances and Smallholder Household Welfare in Nepal
Damodar Gautam
Working Paper, 2025
Using the Household Risk and Vulnerability Survey (HRVS) 2016–2018 panel dataset and a Bartik shift-share instrumental variable strategy, this paper estimates the causal effect of overseas remittances on food security and welfare outcomes among smallholder households in Nepal. Key findings reveal that food insecurity gaps reflect differential coping capacity rather than differential shock exposure, with significant heterogeneity across household types.
