Florida International University (FIU) — Miami, FL

I am a Ph.D. student in Economics at Florida International University. My research spans Applied Macroeconomics, Development Economics, Environmental Economics, and Causal Inference. In macroeconomics, I study how global shocks — commodity prices, demand, and uncertainty — transmit to domestic inflation across heterogeneous economies using Bayesian Structural VAR models. In development economics, I examine how overseas remittances shape food security and household welfare among smallholder households in Nepal using shift-share IV and panel data methods. Prior to FIU, I served as a Research Officer at Nepal's National Agricultural Research Council (NARC) and CIMMYT-NSAF, conducting socio-economic research across production economics, technology adoption, and natural resource management.

Working Paper 2025 Macroeconomics International Economics
Global Shocks and Domestic Inflation: Evidence from 45 Economies
Damodar Gautam
Working Paper · Under Revision for Journal Submission, 2025
This paper examines how global economic shocks — commodity price shocks, global demand shocks, and uncertainty shocks — transmit to domestic inflation across 45 heterogeneous economies. Using a Bayesian Structural VAR (B-SVAR) framework augmented with novel global uncertainty indices, I document significant heterogeneity in transmission intensity driven by trade openness, financial development, central bank independence, exchange rate regimes, and commodity exposure. The findings provide new evidence on the international propagation of inflationary pressures and their structural determinants.

Working Paper 2025 Development Economics Food Security
Overseas Remittances and Smallholder Household Welfare in Nepal
Damodar Gautam
Working Paper · Under Revision for Journal Submission, 2025
Using the Household Risk and Vulnerability Survey (HRVS) 2016–2018 panel dataset and a Bartik shift-share instrumental variable strategy, this paper estimates the causal effect of overseas remittances on food security and welfare outcomes among smallholder households in Nepal. IV-TWFE buffering models and PSM-DiD robustness checks reveal that food insecurity gaps reflect differential coping capacity rather than differential shock exposure, with significant heterogeneity across household types.

Comparative Advantage, Relative Export Competitiveness, and Trade Sustainability of Major Agricultural Commodities in Nepal
Damodar Gautam
Journal of Agriculture and Food Research · Under Review, 2024
This study analyzes the comparative advantage, relative export competitiveness, and trade sustainability of major agricultural commodities in Nepal. In Preparation 2024 Environmental Economics Climate Change
Quantification and Spatio-Temporal Predictive Modeling for Climate Change's Impact on Row Crop Production in the US
Damodar Gautam
Under Preparation, 2024
This paper quantifies and models the spatio-temporal effects of climate change — specifically temperature and precipitation variation — on row crop production across the United States. Integrating econometric methods with machine learning (LSTM networks and ensemble models), the study produces county-level predictive estimates of yield response to climate trajectories, informing adaptation policy for American agriculture.

Determinants of Improved Seed Adoption and Intensity in Lentil Farming: Evidence from Terai Region of Nepal
Damodar Gautam, Shiva Chandra Dhakal, Rishi Ram Kattel & Narayan Prasad Khanal

Published2023Value ChainSeed Systems
Strengthening the Competitiveness of the Lentil Seed System of Nepal: A Value Chain Approach
Damodar Gautam, Shiva Chandra Dhakal, Rishi Ram Kattel & Narayan Prasad Khanal
Journal of Agribusiness and Rural Development, 1(67): 63–77, 2023 · DOI: 10.17306/J.JARD.2023.01640
Using household surveys of 300 grain-producing and 100 seed-producing farmers across Kailali, Dang, and Siraha districts, this study maps Nepal's lentil seed value chain. ROI per total cost was highest for seed producers (59.3%), while total price spread reached 45.21% of consumer price — evidence of systemic marketing inefficiency. The study calls for coordinated policy reforms and public-private partnerships across the value chain.

Published2023Production EconomicsWTP
Willingness to Pay (WTP) for Heat-Tolerant Maize Hybrids in the Mid-Western Terai Region of Nepal
Atul P. Kulkarni, Mahendra P. Tripathi, Damodar Gautam, Keshab B. Koirala, Manoj Kandel, Dhruba Regmi, Sudha Sapkota & Pervez H. Zaidi
Sustainability (MDPI), 15(4): 3068, 2023 · DOI: 10.3390/su15043068
Using the contingent valuation method on a socioeconomic survey of 404 randomly selected maize-growing households in Nepal's Terai, this study estimates farmers' WTP for CIMMYT heat-tolerant maize hybrids. Average WTP is positive but below market price, shaped by farm size, income, information access, and prior exposure to stress-resilient varieties. Findings support public-private investment in climate-adaptive crop deployment.
